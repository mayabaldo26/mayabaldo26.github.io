Who I am

I don't just make things look good. I figure out why they work — then make them work better.

I graduated from Penn State's Smeal College of Business with a marketing degree and a 3.89 GPA, but what I'm most proud of isn't the grade — it's that I spent those years actually doing the work. I interned at a global pharma company, managed social media for a national nonprofit, helped an AI program grow its brand, and built a breakfast campaign for a local brewery that got me hired before I even graduated. I like to be in the room where decisions get made, not just executing tasks. I'm analytical enough to love a good spreadsheet and creative enough to know data alone doesn't move people.

Projects I'm proud of

Antifragile Brewing Co.

The campaign that got me hired

Campaign Strategy

This started as a class project. My team was tasked with creating a marketing plan for a local business — we chose Antifragile, a craft brewery in State College. The challenge: nobody thought of them as a morning spot. I built a full go-to-market strategy to reposition them as an early-hours creative hub for grad students and faculty, with messaging like "Fuel what you're building" and a habit-formation loyalty program. They liked it enough to offer me an internship before the semester ended.

Outcome
Secured a strategic collaboration that drove 400% increase in engagement. Also recruited and hired my own replacement intern.

American Breast Cancer Foundation

A published piece that does real work

Content & Research

During my social media internship at ABCF, I wasn't just scheduling posts — I was researching emerging health topics and writing editorial content that drove awareness and donations. I wrote a piece on the link between alcohol and breast cancer risk, timed to the holiday season when the topic was most relevant. It required real research, a careful tone, and the kind of writing that makes complex information feel approachable without dumbing it down.
